# Topic 1:  Landau Theory of Phase transitions and Magnetism
Introduce order parameters for ferro/antiferromagnetism, the Landau free-energy expansion, symmetry constraints on allowed terms, and how symmetry breaking lifts degeneracies. Explain Kramer’s theorem (time-reversal operator) and how breaking of time-reversal symmetry together with spatial symmetries can lift or protect degeneracies. Conclude by motivating altermagnetism as a symmetry-driven phase distinct from conventional ferro- and antiferromagnetism.

# Topic 2: Spin groups and collinear Altermagnetism
Define spin (magnetic) space groups and how spatial point-group operations combined with spin rotations produce altermagnetic symmetry. Explain the minimal symmetry condition that produces compensated magnetization but nontrivial spin splitting (rotation-related sign changes between sublattices). Discuss classification (collinear vs noncollinear) and illustrate with simple model Hamiltonians and candidate crystal symmetries. Conclude by giving concrete examples of candidate materials.

# Topic 3: Spintronics and Altermagnetism
First, we start with a general introduction to spintronics. Introduce the concept of spin current and the development of spintronics technology. Then specifically altermagnets as a route to nonrelativistic spin splitting without stray fields. Survey device concepts and key phenomena of altermagnetic spintronics.

# Topic 4: Experimental evidence of altermangnetism: ARPES and spin-resolved ARPES
Introduce the experimental techniques. Present the experimental angle resolved photo emission spectroscopy (ARPES) and spin-resolved ARPES evidence for altermagnetic spin splitting: experimental geometry, energy/momentum resolution, what one expects for spin-polarized band dispersions in altermagnets, and how the reported measurements distinguish altermagnetism from other scenarios (SOC, ferromagnetism, antiferromagnetism).

# Topic 5: Coupling of altermagnetism to the lattice: electron–phonon, paramagnon–polaron physics
Introduce lattice dynamics. Explain how the altermagnetic order parameter couples to lattice degrees of freedom: how magnetoelastic coupling changes phonon spectra, how dynamic magnetic fluctuations (paramagnons) hybridize with phonons to produce paramagnon-polarons, and consequences for transport and optical spectra. Present theoretical approaches and experimental signatures (Raman, neutron, inelastic x-ray).

# Topic 6: Designing and tuning materials for altermagnetism
Survey routes to realize, enhance, or switch altermagnetic order: materials selection (symmetry and electronic structure criteria), strain engineering, doping, heterostructure/proximity approaches, and electrical or optical tuning. Include first-principles design strategies and experimental synthesis notes for thin films and heterostructures. Discuss stability, onset temperature, and what experimental knobs can tune the altermagnetic splitting.

# Topic 7: Magnons in Altermagnets: circular dichroism as a probe
Give the theory of spin waves for collinear compensated altermagnets, expected magnon band splittings, lifetime anisotropies, and selection rules. Explain resonant inelastic x-ray scattering (RIXS) and inelastic neutron / spin-resolved techniques for detecting magnon branches; cover circular dichroism in RIXS as a probe. Conclude with the recent systematic mapping of altermagnetic magnons as a case study. 

# Topic 8: Probing magnetism with light: optical and magneto-optical methods (ME)
Review magneto-optical effects (polar / longitudinal Kerr, magnetic circular dichroism), how interband transitions encode spin-dependent band structure, and how bulk optical probes (reflectivity, ellipsometry, infrared conductivity) can detect altermagnetic spin splitting. Discuss experimental protocols, expected signal strengths, and possible confusion with SOC or extrinsic effects.

# Topic 9: Spin-orbit coupling, Zeeman fields, and topological transitions in altermagnets
Discuss the impact of spin-orbit coupling on altermagnets. Show how external Zeeman fields (or internal exchange fields) interact with altermagnetic spin splitting to produce band crossings, gap openings, or topological band inversions (Chern/Weyl nodes, nodal lines). Include simple k.p or tight-binding models that demonstrate how altermagnetic symmetry allows or forbids certain topological features.

# Topic 10: Non-collinear Altermagnetism and Antialtermagnetism
Introduce non-colinear spin ordering, examples, and associated order parameters. Explain the difference between altermagnetism and anti-altermagnetism (broken time-reversal symmetry vs. broken parity) and consequences for electronic bands. Discuss consequences for transport and experimental signatures like non-relativistic Edelstein effect and anomalous Hall effect.

# Topic 11:  Altermagnetic diode effectExplain the basics of superconductivity and critical current
Introduce the superconducting diode effect [1], including the general symmetry requirements (broken time-reversal symmetry and inversion). Why are antiferromagnets not enough? Under which condition can altermagnets lead to a superconducting diode effect [2]? Optional: discuss the proximity effect and how it was used in [3] to recently induce a diode effect in the absence of net magnetization [4]?

# Topic 12: Altermagnets and spin liquids
Introduce the basic concept of a spin liquid (a phase of a spin model, characterized by the loss of magnetic order due to quantum fluctuations, and fractionalized excitations) [1]. Explain how spin liquids can be described by parton theories, where spin operators are fractionalized into bosons or fermions, with additional constraints. Discuss, based on [2], what kind of spin liquid phases are expected to emerge out of or in the vicinity of a square-lattice altermagnet.
