Probing Magnetism with Light

Optical and Magneto-Optical Methods in Altermagnetic Research

Seminar Topic 8

Theoretical Solid State Physics

Key Questions for Today:

  • How does magnetization modify the dielectric tensor?
  • What distinguishes Polar vs. Longitudinal Kerr effects?
  • Can we "see" altermagnetic splitting in the bulk?

1. Macroscopic Origin of MO Effects

The Dielectric Permittivity Tensor

The interaction of light with a magnetic medium is described by the relative permittivity tensor \(\hat{\epsilon}\). For a medium magnetized along \(z\):

\[ \hat{\epsilon} = \begin{pmatrix} \epsilon_{xx} & \epsilon_{xy} & 0 \\ -\epsilon_{xy} & \epsilon_{xx} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \epsilon_{zz} \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Diagonal (\(\epsilon_{xx}\)): Even function of \(\mathbf{M}\). Governs standard refraction/absorption.
  • Off-Diagonal (\(\epsilon_{xy}\)): Odd function of \(\mathbf{M}\). This term is responsible for Faraday and Kerr effects. Sato (2022), Eq. 11

Note: \(\epsilon_{xy}\) is complex: \(\epsilon_{xy} = \epsilon'_{xy} + i\epsilon''_{xy}\). The real and imaginary parts correspond to different physical observables.

Circular Birefringence & Dichroism

Solving Maxwell's equations in the medium leads to two circularly polarized eigenmodes:

Complex Refractive Indices:

\[ \tilde{N}_{\pm}^2 = \epsilon_{xx} \pm i\epsilon_{xy} \]

where \((+)\) is RCP and \((-)\) is LCP.

  • Circular Birefringence: Difference in \(\text{Re}(\tilde{N})\) leads to Rotation.
  • Circular Dichroism: Difference in \(\text{Im}(\tilde{N})\) leads to Ellipticity.
Complex Rotation: \(\Phi = \theta + i\eta\).
Rotation \(\theta \propto \Delta n\), Ellipticity \(\eta \propto \Delta \kappa\).

2. The Magneto-Optical Kerr Effect (MOKE)

Geometry A: Polar Kerr Effect

Magnetization \(\mathbf{M}\) is normal to the reflection surface.

  • Incident linearly polarized light becomes elliptically polarized upon reflection.
  • Rotation \(\theta_K\) and Ellipticity \(\eta_K\) are directly proportional to \(M_z\).

Complex Kerr Rotation:

\[ \Phi_K = \frac{\tilde{r}_+ - \tilde{r}_-}{\tilde{r}_+ + \tilde{r}_-} \approx \frac{-\epsilon_{xy}}{(\epsilon_{xx}-1)\sqrt{\epsilon_{xx}}} \]

Resonance enhancement occurs at the plasma frequency where \(\epsilon_{xx} \to 1\). Sato (2022), Eq. 26

MOKE Geometries (cont.)

Longitudinal Kerr

\(\mathbf{M}\) is parallel to the surface and the plane of incidence.

\[ \Phi_{K,long} \propto \epsilon_{xy} \sin \phi_0 \]

\(\phi_0\): angle of incidence. Vanishes at normal incidence!

Transverse Kerr

\(\mathbf{M}\) is perpendicular to the plane of incidence.

No rotation of polarization. Instead, a change in the reflectivity amplitude for p-polarized light.

\[ \frac{\Delta R}{R} \propto \text{Im}(\epsilon_{xy}) \]
Experimental Choice: Polar MOKE is preferred for thin films with out-of-plane anisotropy; Longitudinal for in-plane magnets.

Magnetic Circular Dichroism (MCD)

MCD measures the difference in absorption coefficients \(\alpha\) for RCP and LCP light.

\[ \Delta \alpha = \alpha_+ - \alpha_- = \frac{4\pi}{\lambda} \text{Im}(\tilde{N}_+ - \tilde{N}_-) \]
  • Microscopic View: MCD probes the difference in transition probabilities between spin-split sub-bands.
  • Sum Rules: Orbital and spin magnetic moments can be extracted by integrating MCD spectra over absorption edges (common in X-ray XMCD).
  • In Altermagnets: MCD can detect staggered magnetization even if the net \(\mathbf{M}=0\), provided the symmetry allows coupling to the light's angular momentum.

3. Microscopic Origins

The Role of SOC and Exchange Splitting

Why does \(\epsilon_{xy}\) exist? It requires two ingredients:

  1. Exchange Splitting (\(\Delta_{ex}\)): Creates a population difference between spin-up and spin-down states.
  2. Spin-Orbit Coupling (SOC): Links the spin to the orbital motion, allowing light (which couples to orbits) to "feel" the spin.

Quantum Mechanical Expression:

\[ \epsilon_{xy}(\omega) \propto \sum_{n,m} \frac{f_{nm}^+ - f_{nm}^-}{\omega_{nm}^2 - \omega^2} \]

\(f_{nm}^\pm\): Oscillator strengths for RCP/LCP transitions. Sato (2022), Eq. 50

4. Bulk Optical Probes

Reflectivity and Optical Conductivity

Beyond MOKE, standard optical probes provide vital information about the band structure symmetry.

  • Drude Weight: Probes the density of itinerant carriers.
  • Interband Transitions: Peaks in \(\sigma_1(\omega)\) correspond to specific transitions in the Brillouin zone.
  • Pauli Edge: A sharp cutoff in conductivity indicating the Fermi level position relative to band crossings.

Case Study: RuO\(_2\)

Broadband IR spectroscopy showed a Pauli edge at 0.1 eV, perfectly matching a non-magnetic Dirac nodal line model. Wenzel et al. (2025)

5. Detecting Altermagnetic Splitting

The Challenge of Anisotropy

Altermagnets (like \(\kappa\)-Cl or MnTe) have lower symmetry. Standard MOKE formulas for isotropic crystals fail.

The \(2 \times 2\) Reflectance Matrix Approach:

\[ \mathbf{E}_{refl} = \begin{pmatrix} \tilde{r}_{xx} & \tilde{r}_{xy} \\ \tilde{r}_{yx} & \tilde{r}_{yy} \end{pmatrix} \mathbf{E}_{inc} \]

In orthorhombic altermagnets, \(\tilde{r}_{xx} \neq \tilde{r}_{yy}\). This "linear dichroism" can be 1000x larger than the MO signal! Iguchi et al. (2025)

  • Protocol: Use PEM modulation and careful sample rotation to isolate the field-odd \(\tilde{r}_{xy}\) from the field-even anisotropy.

Altermagnetism vs. SOC

How do we know the splitting is "Altermagnetic"?

  • Energy Scale: Altermagnetic splitting is driven by Exchange (\(J\)), often \(\sim 100\) meV. SOC splitting in light elements is \(\sim 1\) meV.
  • Temperature Dependence: MO signals in altermagnets disappear exactly at \(T_N\).
  • Field Dependence: Iguchi et al. (2025) showed that MOKE in \(\kappa\)-Cl is non-linear with field, proving it arises from the altermagnetic order parameter, not just canted moments.

Summary & Conclusion

  • MOKE and MCD are powerful probes of the spin-polarized JDOS.
  • Macroscopic MO effects are encoded in the off-diagonal permittivity \(\epsilon_{xy}\).
  • Bulk probes (\(\sigma(\omega)\)) can rule out altermagnetism by detecting topological features like Dirac nodal lines (e.g., RuO\(_2\)).
  • New Protocols: Anisotropic reflectance matrix analysis is required for non-cubic altermagnets.
