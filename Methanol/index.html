Molecular Dynamics (MD)

Classical & ML Potentials

Iterative calculation of atomic interactions to predict system behavior over time using the equation of motion derived from Newton's second law:

$$ m \frac{d^2 \mathbf{r}}{dt^2} = -\nabla V(\mathbf{r}) $$

  • NVT Ensemble: Constant Number of particles, Volume, and Temperature, controlled via a Langevin thermostat (later Nose-Hoover) which adds stochastic terms.
  • Numerical Integration: Algorithms like the Verlet method iteratively update positions and velocities based on recalculated forces.
  • Ergodic Hypothesis: Connects time averages from trajectories to ensemble averages, allowing for the calculation of thermodynamic properties.

Density Functional Theory (DFT)

Electronic Structure

Describes many-body systems via electron density \(\rho(\mathbf{r})\) rather than wavefunctions, utilizing the Kohn-Sham equations to find a self-consistent field: $$ V_{s}(\mathbf{r}) = V_{\text{ext}}(\mathbf{r}) + V_H(\mathbf{r}) + V_{\text{xc}}(\mathbf{r}) $$

  • Hohenberg-Kohn Theorems: Ground-state properties are uniquely determined by the electron density through a universal functional \(E[\rho]\).
  • Approximations: Necessary due to complexity; includes LCAO, the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and exchange-correlation functionals (LDA, GGA).

Dispersion Correction (D3)

Capturing Long-Range Effects

Standard DFT often fails to capture van der Waals interactions. The Grimme-D3 method adds an empirical energy term to the DFT total energy: \[ E_{\text{disp}} = - \sum_{i<j} \left( \frac{C_6^{ij}}{R_{ij}^6} f_{d,6}(R_{ij}) + \frac{C_8^{ij}}{R_{ij}^8} f_{d,8}(R_{ij}) \right) \]

  • Pairwise Additive: Based on atom-pair coefficients \((C_n^{ij})\) and distances \((R_{ij})\).
  • Damping Functions: Critical for preventing the correction from diverging at short interatomic distances where DFT is already effective.
https://pubs.aip.org/aip/jcp/article/132/15/154104/926936/A-consistent-and-accurate-ab-initio

Neural Network Foundations

Deep Learning for PES

Utilizes stacked layers of artificial neurons to approximate complex potential energy surfaces (PES). Each layer performs matrix operations followed by a non-linear activation (e.g., ReLU):
Stacked layers perform matrix operations with non-linear activation: $$ \mathbf{a}^{(l)} = f(\mathbf{W}^{(l)} \cdot \mathbf{a}^{(l-1)} + \mathbf{b}^{(l)}) $$

  • Local Atomic Partitioning: The total energy is the sum of contributions from individual atoms based on their local environment: \(E_{\text{total}} = \sum_i E_i\).
  • Descriptors (GMNN): Transforms geometry into numerical vectors invariant under translation, rotation, and permutation.
  • Automatic Differentiation: Forces are obtained analytically from the energy prediction, ensuring thermodynamic stability.

Uncertainty Quantification (UQ)

Shallow Ensembles

Assessing model confidence using an ensemble with a shared backbone but randomly initialized last layers. Uncertainty is calculated as the variance across predictions:
\[ \sigma_x = \sqrt{\frac{1}{M-1} \sum_{m=1}^{M} (x_m - \bar{x})^2} \]

  • Active Learning: High variance \(\sigma_x\) identifies configurations where the model is unconfident, signaling a need for new DFT training data.
  • Stability: Monitoring uncertainty during MD runs prevents simulations from entering non-physical regions of the configuration space.
https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jctc.0c00347

MACE sampling: Running a 1ns MD simulation with a foundation model to explore phase space.

MGRID optimization: Finding optimal real-space grid convergence to avoid noise in training data.

DFT + D3 relabeling: High-accuracy ab initio labeling with PBE functional and Grimme D3 dispersion corrections.

MLIP training: Training a custom Gaussian Moment Neural Network tailored for methanol dynamics.

Production MD: Running large-scale simulations with the trained MLIP and ensemble-based uncertainty quantification.

RDF and diffusion: Computing radial distribution functions and self-diffusion coefficients against experimental data.

Why a Workflow Manager?

Beyond Bash Scripts

Using ZnTrack and IPSuite provides object-oriented management of complex simulation chains (MD → DFT → ML).

  • Reproducibility: Every node (DFT, training) is cached; nodes only re-run if inputs or parameters change.
  • Scalability: Easily expand from one box of methanol to different temperatures or densities.
  • Traceability: Data-Version Control (DVC) links every output (trajectory, ML model) to a specific code commit.

Foundation Models

MACE is pre-trained on diverse datasets. We use it to sample 1ns of MD cheaply to explore phase space before relabeling.

Why Train a Custom MLIP?

While MACE is general, a tailored model ensures better accuracy for methanol's specific dynamics, like self-diffusion and hydrogen bonding.

Simulations are conducted at 300K, ensuring density matches experimental liquid methanol values.

Functional Selection & D3

Exchange-Correlation Functional

We use PBE, a common GGA functional. It is a robust balance between accuracy and computational efficiency.

Dispersion (D3)

Standard DFT fails at long-range van der Waals forces. We add Grimme D3 to correctly capture intermolecular attraction.

  • GGA: Works well for molecules because it accounts for electron density gradients
  • LDA: Only accounts for electron density, which is less accurate for molecules but fine for metals
  • Evaluating D3 outside CP2K (via TorchDFTD3) allows for faster evaluation and easier decomposition of dispersion contributions.

DFT Convergence: MGRID

The MGRID parameter in CP2K controls the real-space grid cutoff for Hartree and XC potentials.

  • Goal: Find the balance between accuracy and computational cost.
  • Threshold: Variation in forces and energies must be < 0.002 units.
  • Result: Optimal cutoff prevents "grid noise" from polluting the MLIP training data.
  • Conclusion: A cutoff of \(500 \text{ Ry}\) provides a good balance.

Production MD Setup

  • Relaxation: Before MD, we use ASEGeoOpt to minimize forces to prevent unphysical behavior.
  • Timestep: The timestep has to resolve high-frequency O-H vibrations while maintaining stability, which is given for 0.5 femtoseconds.
  • Scaling: Box size increased to 64 molecules for better statistics.

Observation

Model ensembles are used here to detect if the simulation enters "unseen" configurations where uncertainty spikes. Also, we can determine an uncertainty quantification by looking at the spread of predictions across the ensemble, which share a common backbone but differ in their initialization of the final layer. Also a force threshold of 2 eV/Å is set to detect unphysical configurations. We don't use an energy threshold because the energy of the system is systemsize dependent.

Summary

  • Workflow: Unified management ensures every simulation step is linked and reproducible.
  • Labeling: MGRID convergence and GGA+D3 provide a high-quality physical baseline.
  • Training: Hyperparameter tuning and ensembles minimize error and provide uncertainty.
  • Production: The final MLIP successfully runs stable simulations but fails to reproduce methanol's structural (RDF) and dynamic (Diffusion) properties at ab initio levels.

Improvements:

  • Neural Network: Improved model selection and hyperparameter optimization
  • Dispersion Corrections: Improved parameter setting
  • Training Dataset: Expanded with more diverse configurations
  • Classical Force Fields: Comparison as sampling MD simulations against MACE
